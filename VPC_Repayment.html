BBVA × VPC — Repayment Analysis

Prepared: April 28, 2026 Source: BigQuery · finance_tapes_global Market: Colombia USD/COP spot: 3,650 · Hedge FWD: 3,855

Habi is refinancing its Renta 4 VPC portfolio (USD 37M outstanding, 1,144 active units) into a new BBVA-COL credit line (COP 151B total facility, target drawdown April 30, 2026).

Of the 1,144 VPC units, 808 are BBVA-eligible (616 confirmed CTL, 73 estimated, 119 Sellers pending), representing COP 123.7B in collateral. The base case assumes a 751-NID contribution (≈ COP 114.1B) supporting a maximum BBVA disbursement of COP 97.5B at the 1.17× borrowing base. Commercially Sold units with valid CTLs are included as eligible collateral (250 eligible of 409 total).

Combined with COP 40.0B available in the VPC Trust, total sources reach COP 137.5B. Total transaction costs — principal repayment, hedge mark-to-market loss, prepayment penalty, and closing costs — amount to COP 154.2B, resulting in a net funding shortfall of −COP 16.69B (−USD 4.57M). Sources fall short of repayment costs and must be bridged.

Figures based on latest CxC Eligibility Approval and Cash on Trust as of April 28, 2026.

3. BBVA Eligibility Framework

The BBVA line requires each contributed property to hold a valid Certificado de Tradición y Libertad (CTL) issued within 180 days of the target drawdown of April 30, 2026 — meaning any CTL dated before November 1, 2025 is stale and renders the unit ineligible. Commercially Sold units with valid CTLs are included as eligible collateral.

Eligibility Assumptions

  • Target drawdown date: April 30, 2026
  • CTL validity window: CTL must be dated on or after November 1, 2025 (180-day lookback)
  • Median deed → CTL lag: 28 days (based on historical Colombia portfolio)
  • Sellers stage: All 119 units included — they will receive a fresh CTL at deed signing and contribution to the line
  • Pre Listing, Listed: Eligible if (a) confirmed CTL ≥ Nov 1 2025, OR (b) no CTL yet but deed + 28d falls within the window
  • Commercially Sold: Included if CTL is confirmed ≥ Nov 1 2025, OR estimated CTL in window (deed + 28d between Nov 1 2025 and Apr 30 2026)
  • CTL too old: Units with confirmed CTL before Nov 1, 2025 are excluded

Sensitivity — Effect of Excluding Near-Term CS Closers

The BBVA line has a ~1-year no-prepay grace period (amortization begins ~May 2027). Contributing a home that sells within that window produces trapped cash: sale proceeds flow to a BBVA sweep account (retaining the ~0.855 advance against that home) and cannot be used to retire debt until amortization kicks in — meanwhile Habi keeps paying 17% interest on the drawn principal.

The corrected economics below compare lifetime treasury value over the 1-year grace period, assuming Habi bridges the at-close funding gap of excluded units with short-term financing at 10% until sale proceeds arrive (~30 days for ≤30d cohort, ~45 days average for ≤60d cohort).

Year-1 Interest Savings = (excluded value × 1 ÷ 1.17) × 17% × 1 year, reflecting interest avoided on the BBVA advance that would have been drawn against the excluded units and held locked for the full grace period. At-Close Shortfall Bridge = base shortfall plus lost BBVA advance, which must be funded externally until excluded homes sell. Bridge Cost = shortfall × 10% × avg days until sale. Both options ultimately deliver COP 1.22 per peso of home value to Habi; the trade-off is financing carry vs. bridge financing.

Decision hinges on bridge availability. The "Exclude" scenarios only work if Habi can secure short-term bridge financing at ~10% (treasury, short bank line, or extended VPC window). If no bridge is available, the at-close shortfall forces Option A (include all) regardless of the 1-year carry cost. A parallel workstream to secure COP 20–30B of flexible short-term capacity would unlock the savings.
